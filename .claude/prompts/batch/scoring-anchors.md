# Scoring Anchors — Reference Examples

Use these concrete examples to calibrate your scores. If you're unsure, compare the LP you're reviewing to these anchors.

---

## Score 1-2/10 — Poor (Critical Issues)

**Marketing Examples:**
- No visible CTA anywhere on the page
- Value proposition completely unclear or missing
- Copy that actively misleads or confuses
- Wall of text with no hierarchy or scanning points

**UX Examples:**
- Text unreadable (wrong contrast, tiny font, overlapping elements)
- Broken layout with horizontal scrolling on mobile
- Interactive elements impossible to tap (<30px)
- Content cut off or hidden without scroll indication

**Brand Examples:**
- Colors and style completely wrong for the sector (funeral home in neon pink)
- Tone that actively damages trust (formal B2B service with slang)
- Visual chaos that screams "unprofessional"
- Stock imagery that contradicts the brand message

---

## Score 3-4/10 — Below Average (Noticeable Problems)

**Marketing Examples:**
- CTA exists but is weak ("Submit", "Click here") with no urgency
- Benefits mentioned but buried in long paragraphs
- Generic copy that could apply to any competitor
- Trust signals absent or unconvincing

**UX Examples:**
- Inconsistent spacing (some sections cramped, others too sparse)
- Font sizes that don't establish clear hierarchy
- Color palette functional but uninspired
- Touch targets work but feel cramped

**Brand Examples:**
- Design "acceptable" but forgettable for the sector
- Tone inconsistent (formal in header, casual in body)
- Stock photos that feel generic rather than brand-specific
- Would not stand out among competitors

---

## Score 5-6/10 — Average (Functional but Forgettable)

**This is your default starting point. Most AI-generated LPs land here.**

**Marketing Examples:**
- CTA present with action verb but no compelling reason to click NOW
- Value proposition visible but not differentiated
- Adequate social proof (testimonials) but generic
- Conversion path exists but has friction points

**UX Examples:**
- Layout works on mobile without issues
- Typography readable but using safe defaults
- Adequate spacing — nothing jarring, nothing delightful
- Would not screenshot this as a design reference

**Brand Examples:**
- Colors match the sector broadly (blue for B2B, warm for food)
- Tone appropriate but could be any company in the sector
- Professional appearance but no personality
- "Template feel" — swap the logo and it still works

---

## Score 7-8/10 — Good (Solid Work, Some Excellence)

**Marketing Examples:**
- CTA with compelling copy AND visual prominence AND clear benefit
- Value proposition that specifically addresses target audience pain
- Urgency elements that feel authentic, not manipulative
- Copy that sounds like a human, not a template

**UX Examples:**
- Thoughtful visual hierarchy that guides the eye naturally
- Typography choices that add personality while staying readable
- Spacing with intentional rhythm and breathing room
- Mobile experience that feels native, not adapted

**Brand Examples:**
- Design choices that feel specifically RIGHT for this brand/sector
- Tone that has personality while staying appropriate
- Visual elements that reinforce the brand story
- Would remember this LP if shown again tomorrow

---

## Score 9-10/10 — Exceptional (Rarely Given)

**Reserve these scores for work you would genuinely recommend as an example to follow.**

**Marketing Examples:**
- Immediately clear why THIS service/product, why NOW, why HERE
- CTA you actually want to click (curiosity + benefit + low friction)
- Copy that surprises or delights while staying on-message
- Would convert skeptical visitors

**UX Examples:**
- Micro-interactions and polish that show craft
- Layout that feels innovative yet intuitive
- Would screenshot for design inspiration
- Competes with award-winning sites (Awwwards level)

**Brand Examples:**
- Feels like a real brand's professional agency work
- Emotional resonance — you FEEL something viewing it
- Unique personality impossible to confuse with competitors
- Would recommend as a best-in-class sector example

---

## Quick Calibration Test

Before submitting, ask yourself:

| If I gave... | Then I should have... |
|--------------|----------------------|
| All 5s and 6s | A very generic LP with no standout features AND no serious flaws |
| A 9 or 10 | Something I'd screenshot to show colleagues as excellent work |
| A 2 or 3 | A clear, specific deficiency I can point to |
| Average > 7 | Genuine excellence across multiple dimensions (rare) |
| Average < 4 | Multiple serious issues that harm the LP's effectiveness |

**If your scores don't match these expectations, recalibrate.**
